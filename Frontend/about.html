About PhishDefender

A real-time phishing detection system combining machine learning and explainable AI to identify and explain suspicous emails.

Developed as part of Cybersecurity and Digital Forensics Honours Project 2025/26

Introduction

Phishing remains one of the most persisten cybersecuirty threats today. Research shows that over 40% of organsiations experience phishing attempts daily, with moer than 75% facing attacks each month. Around 30% of phishing emails are opened, demonstrating the continued effectiveness of social engineering techniques.

Traditional detection methods struggle to keep pace. nearly two thirds of phishing campigns last under two hours, meaning many attacks disappear before blacklist systems can update. Initial detection rates can fall below 20%, leaving organisations exposed during the most critical early stage.

This project devlops a real-time phishing detection system using machine learning combined with Explainable AI (XAI). By integrating technical and behavioural insights, the system aims to deliver accurate, transparent, and trustworthy phishing detection for modern email enviroments.

(De Bona & Paci, 2020; Sheng et al. 2009; Tanimu & Shiaeles, 2022)

Purpose

The aim of this project is to design and evaluate a real-time phishing detection system capable of accuratly classifying malicous emails while also providing transparent, interpretable explanations for each decision.

Importnace

By combining adaptive machine learning with explaianbility and behavioural insight, this system aims to improve trust, accountability, and practical deploymentof phishing detection in modern email enviroments.
